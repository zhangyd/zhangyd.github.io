Delivice LLC

Co-Founder and Manager

While attending college at the University of Michigan, a couple friends and I frequently found it difficult to cook for ourselves. Constantly eating out was expensive and unhealthy, but going grocery shopping was time consuming and inconvenient without a car.

This dilemma inspired us to co-found Delivice LLC, a grocery delivery service for U of M students and Ann Arbor residents who want to cook their own meals but lack the time or convenient transportation.

From initial market validation to building the platform and fulfilling orders, my team and I have turned Delivice from a bare idea into a successful small business in the past two years. The company has served over 5,000 orders to date, and we are excited to soon expand to other college towns!

Detective-V

Ruby on Rails App

This is a yearlong design project sponsored by Groupon, a worldwide e-commerce marketplace connecting millions of subscriber to local deals.

Like many other companies, Groupon leverages open source projects in their code base. Many of these projects have published vulnerabilities that can be patched, but without an easy way to manage all the dependencies, vulnerable ones sometimes go unnoticed, and a single exploitation could cost a company of Groupon’s size millions of dollars.

To tackle this problem, I worked with a multidisciplinary team to develop a Ruby on Rails web application that enables security managers to quickly identify and track vulnerable dependencies across a large number of projects. It manages a pipeline of static analysis tools and support vulnerability scanning for projects built in Ruby, Java, and JavaScript.

CareNET

Android App

Every year, distracted driving causes over 1.6 million crashes and thousands of deaths. Among these accidents, texting and driving is especially prevalent. A simple reason for this is that people simply don’t know when their family or friends are driving and therefore unknowingly text them when they are on the road.

To tackle this problem, a friend and I worked on an Android application that inform users of their friends’ driving statuses. For smartphone users who don’t want to distract their families or friends when they are driving, CareNET is a social-networking app that allows users to see if the people they want to text are driving or not and where they are. Unlike existing anti-texting-and-driving apps that solely blocks incoming texts for drivers, CareNET shifts the responsibility to the non-drivers while also providing the additional value of connecting people based on their shared statuses and locations.

Schools as Open Systems Study

REU

Teachers, principals, and other school employees all know that schools do not exist in isolation. Schools are of a broader network, and they rely on connections to other organizations, such as non-profits, museums, libraries, and corporations, for resources from classroom supplies to updates about events in the neighborhood.

This research project is funded by the National Science Foundation and aims to understand how different types of schools in three different neighborhoods in Chicago interact with their external communities and the implications on the resources they have available for their students.

To help school officials better visualize the research data and utilize some of the initial findings, I prototyped a web application that allows users to query and filter survey data as well as key learnings. I also calculated and studied various network measures for the different communication and resource acquisition networks for each school to help explain the differences in cost and benefit associated with working with external organizations for the surveyed schools.
